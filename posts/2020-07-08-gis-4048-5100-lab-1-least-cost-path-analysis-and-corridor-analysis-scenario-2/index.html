GIS 4048/5100 - Lab 1 Least Cost Path Analysis and Corridor Analysis: Scenario 2

By Kristin

July 8, 2020

For this section of Lab 1, a suitability assessment for property development was performed in an area of Southern Oregon. First, information provided about soil and land types via raster images were reclassified into rasters with cell values indicating suitability ratings for development. Next, suitability ratings were assigned to slope values, using information from a DEM. Following this, rasters were generated indicating the distance from rivers and roads in this study area and these were also reclassified to suitability ratings. These “suitability rasters” were combined using ArcGIS Pro’s weighted overlay tool to produce two different total suitability assessments: first they were combined, but equally weighted at 20% each. This option is shown in the map below on the left. Next, they were combined with slope values rated at 40%, proximity to roads and streams at 10%, and soil and land cover ratings at 20% each. This option is shown below on the right.
