Portfolio

Here is a a sampling of my GIS work:

This map shows the schools in Ward 7 of Washington DC. Elementary, middle, and high schools are indicated by symbols of different size. The main feature of the map - Ward 7 - is a lighter color than the background of the rest of DC to create good figure/ground contrast.

Map of Marathon, Florida created by me using Adobe Illustrator, and the ArcGIS extension for Adobe Illustrator.

Map of average annual precipitation in Washington State from 1981-2010 using hyposometric tinting with 10 classed values and contour lines for clarity. Precipitation values were calculated outside of locations with measured point values using the PRISM method.

This map shows categorized Land Use / Land Cover classifications for this region of Pascagoula, MS and whether or not the classification stood up to verification against the imagery available on Google StreetView at several points.

This map indicates the best-suited corridor for black bears to travel from the two shown areas of Coronado National Forest. Criteria included in the suitability calculation were: elevation, distance from roads, and land cover types. The darker-colored purple corridor indicates the lowest cost (most suitable) corridor and the lighter-colored areas of the corridor indicate the higher-cost (less-suitable) areas of the corridor.

This map shows a comparison between which regions and then specifically which structures are predicted to be flooded by a 1-meter storm surge in Collier County, FL, based on a newer high-resolution Lidar shapemodel and an older, standard USGS DEM.
